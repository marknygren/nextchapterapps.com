Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 15, 2026

Next Chapter Education LLC (“we,” “us,” “our”) builds educational mobile applications. This policy explains what personal information we collect, how we use it, who we share it with, how long we keep it, and your rights. We take children’s privacy seriously and comply with the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) in apps used by children under 13.

Data we collect

We design our apps to collect as little personal information as possible, and what we collect varies by app. Below we describe what we collect using the categories defined by the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), which also cover the categories used in other U.S. state privacy laws.

Categories we may collect

  • Identifiers (CCPA Category A). Email address, if you choose to create an optional account in an app that offers accounts. Persistent device identifiers provided by Apple (such as the Identifier for Vendors) used for app functionality and error diagnostics. IP address when your device communicates with our servers.
  • Internet or other electronic network activity (CCPA Category F). Information about how you interact with our apps — which features are used, when, and for how long — in an aggregated and anonymized form.
  • Inferences (CCPA Category K). Learning progress information (scores, streaks, completed lessons, quiz results) used solely within the app to personalize your experience.

Categories we do not collect

We do not knowingly collect any of the following:

  • Government-issued identifiers, Social Security numbers, driver’s license numbers
  • Financial account numbers or payment card data (in-app purchases, if any, are processed by Apple)
  • Health or medical information
  • Precise geolocation data
  • Biometric identifiers such as fingerprints, face scans, voiceprints, or DNA
  • Audio or video recordings of users
  • Demographic information such as race, ethnicity, religion, or sexual orientation
  • Contents of your mail, email, or text messages

How we collect it

We collect information directly from you when you use our apps, and from Apple’s platform services for technical data such as crash reports and aggregated App Store analytics.

How we use personal information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide and operate our apps
  • Save your learning progress and, if you have an account, sync it across your devices
  • Diagnose bugs, improve performance, and add new features
  • Respond to support and privacy requests
  • Comply with legal obligations

We do not use personal information for third-party advertising, for cross-context behavioral advertising, or for any purpose incompatible with those listed above.

Who we share personal information with

We share personal information only with the following categories of recipients, and only as necessary for the purposes described in this policy:

  • Apple Inc. — for crash reporting, App Store and TestFlight operation, and aggregated app analytics. Apple’s use of this data is governed by Apple’s privacy policy.
  • Service providers and contractors — companies that host our servers or provide technical infrastructure. They are contractually limited to using the data only to provide services to us and are required to maintain its confidentiality.

Sale and sharing of personal information

We do not sell personal information as defined by the CCPA or other U.S. state privacy laws, and we do not share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. This applies equally to data from children under 13, adults, and residents of any state.

In the preceding 12 months, we have not sold personal information and have not shared personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. In the same period, we have disclosed personal information for business purposes only to the categories of recipients listed in the section above.

Sensitive personal information

We do not collect sensitive personal information as defined by the CCPA. That category includes Social Security numbers, driver’s license numbers, financial account numbers, precise geolocation, racial or ethnic origin, religious beliefs, union membership, health information, genetic data, biometric identifiers, sex-life or sexual-orientation data, and the contents of mail, email, or text messages. Because we do not collect this information, we do not use or disclose it for any purpose.

How long we keep data

We retain personal information for the following periods:

  • Account email (if you create an account): kept while your account exists; deleted within 30 days of account deletion, or sooner on request.
  • Learning progress tied to an account: same as above.
  • Learning progress stored locally on the device (no account): kept only on the device and deleted when you delete the app.
  • Anonymous, aggregated usage data: retained for up to 24 months in a form that cannot reasonably identify you.
  • Crash reports and technical platform data: retained by Apple under Apple’s privacy policy.

We delete or anonymize personal information when it is no longer needed for the purposes for which it was collected.

How we protect data

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect personal information against loss, misuse, and unauthorized access. No system is perfectly secure, but we design our apps to minimize the data we hold, which minimizes the data at risk.

Children under 13 (COPPA)

We comply with the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), including the amendments finalized by the FTC with a compliance deadline of April 22, 2026.

What we collect from children

In apps designed for children, we collect only what is needed for the core app experience:

  • Learning progress (scores, streaks, completed lessons, quiz results), stored on the device
  • Persistent identifiers used solely to support internal operations — namely, providing the app’s functionality, diagnosing errors, maintaining security, and protecting against fraud

What we do not collect from children

  • Name, email address, phone number, or street address
  • Photos, videos, or audio recordings
  • Precise geolocation data
  • Biometric identifiers (fingerprints, face scans, voiceprints, DNA, gait patterns)
  • Government-issued identifiers
  • Any other personal information beyond what is strictly necessary for the app to function

Third-party disclosures and parental consent

The only third-party recipient of children’s data is Apple Inc., and only for the purposes of crash reporting, App Store and TestFlight operation, and aggregated analytics.

We do not share children’s personal information with any third party for targeted advertising, profiling, or any other purpose unrelated to the internal operations of the app. If our practices change, we will obtain separate verifiable parental consent before disclosing any child’s personal information to a third party for a new purpose, as required under the amended COPPA Rule.

Parents’ rights

Parents of children using our apps may:

  • Review the personal information we have collected from their child
  • Request deletion of their child’s personal information
  • Refuse to permit any further collection or use of their child’s personal information

To exercise these rights, email us at contact@nextchapterapps.com. We respond within 30 days.

Your rights

If you are a resident of California or another U.S. state with a comprehensive consumer privacy law — including Colorado, Connecticut, Delaware, Florida, Indiana, Iowa, Kentucky, Maryland, Minnesota, Montana, Nebraska, New Hampshire, New Jersey, Oregon, Rhode Island, Tennessee, Texas, Utah, or Virginia — you have the rights listed below. We honor these rights for all U.S. residents regardless of state of residence.

  • Right to know / access. Obtain the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you, the sources, the purposes, the categories of third parties we share it with, and the categories disclosed for business purposes.
  • Right to delete. Delete personal information we have collected from you, subject to limited legal exceptions.
  • Right to correct. Correct inaccurate personal information we maintain about you.
  • Right to portability. Receive your personal information in a portable, readily usable format.
  • Right to opt out of sale or sharing. We do not sell or share personal information, so this right is effectively honored by default.
  • Right to opt out of profiling that produces legal or similarly significant effects. We do not engage in such profiling.
  • Right to limit use of sensitive personal information. We do not collect sensitive personal information, so this right is effectively honored by default.
  • Right to non-discrimination. We will not deny you service, charge different prices, or provide a lesser quality of service because you exercised any of these rights.

How to exercise your rights

Email contact@nextchapterapps.com with your request. We respond within 45 days. To protect your information, we may ask for information sufficient to verify your identity; we will use that information only for verification.

Account deletion. If an app allows you to create an account in the app, it also allows you to delete that account from within the app. You may alternatively email us.

Authorized agents. California residents may designate an authorized agent to make a request on their behalf. We may require the agent to provide proof of authorization and may require you to verify your own identity with us directly.

Global Privacy Control (GPC)

If you send a Global Privacy Control signal through a supported browser or browser extension, we treat it as a request to opt out of the sale and sharing of personal information. Because we do not sell or share personal information, this signal does not change our practices, but we honor it as required by applicable law.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with a new “Last updated” date. Material changes will also be communicated through the apps themselves.
